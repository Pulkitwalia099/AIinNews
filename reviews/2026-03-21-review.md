### Recommendation 1: Fix cross-day article deduplication in ingestion pipeline
**Type:** bug_fix | **Priority:** high | **Effort:** small

**Why:** Two separate readers independently complained that the same stories appear day over day (IDs 10, 11), and the Britannica lawsuit appeared as two distinct URLs in the same week's bottom list — confirming a deduplication gap at both the URL-normalization and cross-day levels. This is directly eroding perceived editorial quality and is likely contributing to the 48% approval rate.

**What to build:** Implement a persistent seen-URLs store (e.g., a simple SQLite table or Redis set) that normalizes URLs before insertion — strip trailing slashes, query parameters, and UTM tags — and rejects any article whose normalized URL was published in the last 7 days. **Expected impact:** Eliminates the duplicate-story complaint, reduces thumbs-down from repeated content, and should lift approval rate by 5-10 percentage points within two weeks.
